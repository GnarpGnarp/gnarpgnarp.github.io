Protien

Proteins are essential molecules made up of smaller units called amino acids. They play crucial roles in our bodies, from building and repairing tissues to regulating processes like metabolism and immune response. Found in a variety of foods such as meat, fish, eggs, and plant-based sources like beans and nuts, proteins are vital for maintaining our health and supporting various bodily functions.


Amiino Acids

Amiino Acids are small chains of molecules that are the makeup of protien, there are 20 different amiino acids although they all share the same basic structure, this structure being made of Carbon, Hydrogen, Nitrogen and Oxygen, Oxygen only being found in the carboxyl group of amiino acid. Among these 20 there is a group called "essential amiino acids" this is a group of 8 which are given such reverence as they cannot be produced within the human body and therefore must be taken in through the diet. The namees of these is as follows:

  1. Isoleucine
  2. Leucine
  3. Lysine
  4. Methionine
  5. Phenylalanine
  6. Threonine
  7. Tryptophan
  8. Valine

Lipoproteins

A lipoprotein is a complex molecule made up of fats (lipids) and proteins. These molecules play a vital role in transporting fats through the bloodstream. The structure of lipoproteins allows them to carry fats, such as cholesterol. They come in two varieties, High Density Lipoproteins and Low Density Lipoproteins. High Lipoproteins are used to carry fats around the body, but they are not made equal. Low Density Lipoproteins (LDL) are often refered to as "bad" lipoproteins due when there are too many LDLs they will begin to line the walls of arteries, restricting the blood flow through them. This can lead to heart disease if it gets too bad. High Density Lipoproteins (HDL) are often refered to as the "good" lipoproteins, this is because as in opposition to the LDLs, they help remove excess cholesterol from the blood stream.


Sources

In a standard diet, sources of protein are plentiful and diverse, offering a range of options to meet the recommended daily amount. Animal-based sources such as poultry, beef, pork, fish, eggs, and dairy products like milk, cheese, and yogurt are rich in high-quality protein and provide essential amino acids necessary for bodily functions. Plant-based sources like beans, lentils, chickpeas, tofu, nuts and seeds also contribute significant amounts of protein to the diet. These are viable sources for those who do not eat meat.


Biological Value

Biological value can be simplified to animal or plant. High biological value means that the source contains the essential amiino acids needed, where as low biological value does not contain these essential amiino acids. There are exceptions however, for example soya beans, despite being plant based are of high biological value.
